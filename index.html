About Us

The 80th Indonesian Independence Day commemorates eight decades of Indonesian independence since its proclamation on August 17, 1945. This momentum reflects the struggle of the heroes in gaining and maintaining independence, as well as an invitation to all Indonesian people to continue to unite, innovate, and build the country towards a more advanced and sovereign direction. In this 80th year, the spirit of nationalism and mutual cooperation is expected to be even stronger in facing the various challenges of the times for the realization of Indonesia Emas 2045.




History

The Independence Day of the Republic of Indonesia (HUT RI) is celebrated every August 17th to commemorate the historic day when Indonesia proclaimed its independence from Dutch and Japanese colonial rule in 1945. The Proclamation of Independence was read by Ir. Soekarno, accompanied by Drs. Mohammad Hatta, at Jalan Pegangsaan Timur No. 56, Jakarta. This moment marked the beginning of the establishment of the Republic of Indonesia as a free, united, sovereign, just, and prosperous nation.

The commemoration of Independence Day is usually filled with various activities such as flag-raising ceremonies, traditional games, cultural performances, and community events. Flag ceremonies are held across the country—at government institutions, schools, and neighborhoods—as a form of respect and gratitude for the independence that has been achieved. In addition, communities decorate their surroundings, raise the national flag (Merah Putih), and participate in festive competitions.

Independence Day also serves as a moment to strengthen the spirit of nationalism, unity, and mutual cooperation among citizens. The younger generation is encouraged to honor the sacrifices of the nation’s heroes and contribute positively to the progress of the country. With the spirit of “Merdeka” (freedom), Indonesians are reminded to continue protecting and developing the nation for a brighter future.
